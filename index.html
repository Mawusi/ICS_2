    Frequently Asked Questions on NDIS

    What is a permanent and significant disability?

    A lifelong disability that significantly impacts an individual’s ability to complete their day to day activities.

    What is early intervention?

    According to the NDIS, early intervention is providing support as early as possible, to a child or an adult, to reduce the impact of developmental delay or disability.

    The goal of early intervention by NDIS is to support an individual’s independence by helping them build their skills. More importantly, providing supports early to reduce the future impact of their disability.

    What is NDIS pre – planning?

    Before an individual has their NDIS planning meeting with their local area coordinator or planner. It is always helpful to evaluate your current needs and living arrangements, before your meeting.

    This will help you understand and make changes in your current situation. By discussing with your NDIS planner, about your goals and aspirations.

    Who is Local Area Coordinator?

    Understanding the NDIS can be overwhelming, and a Local Area Coordinator (also pronounced as L.A.C) work with the NDIS community partners to support an individual applying for NDIS or using their plan to make the most out of it.
